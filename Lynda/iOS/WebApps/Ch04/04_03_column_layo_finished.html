The state of web typography

Typography has dramatically improved on modern browsers. Many special effects can be achieved with CSS rules if you know how to access the right selectors and pseudo classes.

Although @font-face allows you to include fonts on your site, a lot of fonts require special licensing in order to be used online. Another issue is that different browsers and platforms require the fonts to be in different formats. In order to use a custom font on your site, you might need the font on 4 or 5 different formats.

FontSquirrel along with Google’s Font Directory showcase font libraries which you can use on your site free of charge. This is a great way to get started with custom fonts online.

Google’s model makes it easy to incorporate fonts on your site. Google hosts the fonts on their servers, and gives you an extremely easy to use piece of code to put on your site.

“Google’s model makes it easy to incorporate fonts on your site”

FontSquirrel uses the traditional @font-face tag, but provides all the code you’ll need as well as the fonts in all of the necessary formats.

Services like TypeKit, FontDeck allow you to license fonts for use on your sites through differing commercial models. A lot of foundries have gotten smart and have made their fonts available on these services. The quantity of these fonts have increased dramatically in recent years.

While TypeKit gives you an all you can eat model for a fixed price depending on traffic, FontDeck gives you the option to buy a specific font for an annual fee. Both of these sites store the fonts on their own servers.
